How It Began

From a Small Kitchen to a Beloved Shop

In 2010, chef Ana Ferreira returned from five years in Paris with a dream: to bring the beauty of French patisserie together with the soul of Portuguese baking. She opened Casa do Pão in a narrow street in Lisboa with just four tables, a single espresso machine, and an endless passion for craft.

Word spread quickly. Within a year, locals lined up before opening time for the freshly baked croissants, and tourists hunted down the address for what the New York Times called "Lisboa's most beautiful café."

Today, Casa do Pão remains family-run, independent, and deeply rooted in the belief that the best things in life are made slowly, by hand, with love.
